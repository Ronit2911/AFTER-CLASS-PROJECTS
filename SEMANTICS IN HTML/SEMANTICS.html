HOCKEY

Hockey is a fast-paced team sport played by two opposing teams of eleven players who use sticks to hit a ball into the opponent's goal. There are different variations, including field hockey (played on grass or turf) and ice hockey (played on ice with a puck). Field hockey, often considered the national sport of India due to its rich history, is known for its emphasis on skill, strategy, and teamwork. The game requires discipline, cooperation, and is played globally by both men and women in various competitive leagues and international tournaments..

SWIMMING

Swimming is a low-impact, full-body exercise for recreation, fitness, and competition. It involves propelling the body through water using combined arm and leg motions, working cardiovascular and muscular systems to build endurance and strength. Popular styles, or strokes, include freestyle, breaststroke, backstroke, and butterfly. The buoyancy of water reduces stress on joints, making it ideal for individuals with joint conditions, and the activity also provides mental health benefits like stress reduction.

BASEBALL

Baseball is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of nine players. The game involves alternating between batting and fielding, with the offensive team trying to score runs by hitting the ball and advancing around four bases. The defensive team aims to prevent runs by getting batters out. The team with the most runs after nine innings wins. Popular in the United States, Japan, and other parts of the Americas and Asia, it is known for its strategic gameplay, with the pitcher throwing a ball for the batter to hit with a bat.
